About the Artist

Erik Foss is a self-taught, American artist, born in Elgin, Illinois, in 1973. He was raised in the deserts just outside of Phoenix, Arizona. His early inspirations were fueled by the American cultural landscape, a religious upbringing, skateboarding, underground music and other elements from subcultures of the time
Foss has held solo exhibitions at Kaikai Kiki Gallery (Toyko), Castor Gallery (New York), PADRE (New York), Boo-Hooray (Montauk), Paul Loya Gallery (Los Angeles), and Gallery Steinsland Berliner (Stockholm), among others. Select group exhibitions include presentations at Christie's (New York), Great Jones Space (New York), Watermill Foundation 19th Annual Benefit Auction (New York), American Academy of Arts and Letters (New York), Dover Street Market (Los Angeles), and The Hole (New York).
